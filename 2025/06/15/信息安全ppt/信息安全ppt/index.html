Vulnerability - Threat - CounterMeasure

Vulnerability : 一个能被利用并造成系统破坏的薄弱点，是安全系统中的薄弱环节
Threat ：有可能引起系统损失或损害的一系列条件，能够造成伤害的一系列环境
Attack：针对系统的薄弱点，造成危害的一系列具体行为
CounterMeasure：阻止脆弱点带来的危害

Vulnerability: A weakness that can be exploited and cause system damage, a weak link in the security system
Threat: A series of conditions that may cause system loss or damage, a series of environments that can cause harm
Attack: A series of specific actions that target the weaknesses of the system and cause harm
CounterMeasure: Prevent the harm caused by the vulnerability

The water is the threat, the crack the vulnerability, and the finger the control (for now).
How to attack?

CIA-Triad

Confidentiality:一种确保资源只能被授权当事人查看系统的能力
Integrity：一种确保资源只能被授权当事人修改的能力
Availability：一种确保资源只能被授权当事人使用的能力

A capability to ensure that resources can only be modified by authorized parties

  • Authentication：一种能够确认资源或信息所有者身份的系统能力
  • Nonrepudiation：一种能够确认发送者不能对已发送过的信息进行抵赖的系统能力

Cryptography uses mathematical techniques to transform data and prevent it from being read or tampered with by unauthorized parties.That enables exchanging secure messages even in the presence of adversaries.
密码学使用数学技术来转换数据并防止其被未经授权的一方读取或篡改。即使在对手存在的情况下也可以交换安全的消息。

For the purpose of information confidentiality, cryptographic technology is used to process information that needs to be kept confidential during information transmission or storage, so that the processed information cannot be read or interpreted by unauthorized persons (including illegal persons). This process is called encryption.
出于信息保密的目的,在信息传输或存储中,采用密码技术对需要保密的信息进行处理,使得处理后的信息不能被非受权者(含非法者)读懂或解读,这一过程称为加密。

Access Control

Access control is a security technique that regulates who or what can view or use resources in a computing environment. It is a fundamental concept in security that minimizes risk to the business or organization.
访问控制是一种安全技术，它规定了谁或什么可以查看和使用计算系统资源，是安全的基本概念，是企业和组织能降低风险的手段

  • 计算机的安全范shi之一
  • Subject：Human、process、Application发起
  • Object：数据项-接受
  • Mode：访问类型，如读、写、修改等
  • Policy：Authorization that enables a subject to access an object

how to prevent thwart threats and attactks?

To prevent and thwart threats and attacks, it’s essential to implement strong authentication methods like multi-factor authentication (MFA), keep systems updated with regular security patches, segment networks to limit unauthorized access, and train employees to recognize threats such as phishing. Additionally, using intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS), encrypting sensitive data, enforcing strict access control, and having a well-prepared incident response plan are crucial. Regular backups, firewalls, and anti-malware tools also play a key role in mitigating risks and ensuring the integrity of systems and data.

为了防止和抵御威胁与攻击，必须实施强认证方法，如多因素认证（MFA），定期更新系统以修补安全漏洞，分段网络以限制未经授权的访问，培训员工识别钓鱼等威胁。此外，使用入侵检测与防御系统（IDS/IPS）、加密敏感数据、执行严格的访问控制并准备好事件响应计划至关重要。定期备份、防火墙和反恶意软件工具也在减轻风险和确保系统与数据完整性方面发挥重要作用。

Buffer Overflow

  • Occur when data is written beyond the space allocated for it, such as a 10th byte in a 9-byte array.
    当数据写入超出分配空间时发生，例如写入 9 字节数组中的第 10 个字节。

  • In a typical exploitable buffer overflow, an attacker’s inputs are expected to go into regions of memory allocated for data, but those inputs are instead allowed to overwrite memory holding executable code.
    在典型的可利用缓冲区溢出中，攻击者的输入预期会进入分配给数据的内存区域，但这些输入却被允许覆盖保存可执行代码的内存。

  • The trick for an attacker is finding buffer overflow opportunities that lead to overwritten memory being executed, and finding the right code to input.
    攻击者的技巧是找到导致被覆盖内存被执行的缓冲区溢出机会，并找到正确的代码进行输入。

Malware

Race condition：两个或者多个进程同一时间为了争取某个资源造成系统堵塞等情况的竞争状态
Programs planted by an agent with malicious intent to cause unanticipated or undesired effects.
由恶意代理植入的程序，旨在造成意外或不良影响

  • Virus：一种恶意软件，能进行自我复制并将其自身的副本插入到宿主程序当中
    Virus: A type of malware that can replicate itself and insert copies of itself into a host program

  • Worm：A program that spreads copies of itself through a network.
    一种独立运行的恶意软件，不需要宿主程序，会通过网络等途径传播到其他计算机

  • Trojan horse：A malicious program disguised as legitimate software that claims to have some useful function but actually hides malicious functionality. Users may be tricked into downloading or installing a Trojan horse without knowing its true purpose.
    一种伪装成合法软件的恶意程序，它声称具有某种有用的功能，但实际上隐藏着恶意功能。用户可能会被欺骗下载或安装特洛伊木马，而不知道其真实目的。

Security Perimeters （边界）

在每个周边范围内，由于物理控制，您基本上可以控制您的电缆、设备和计算机，所以您不需要太担心保护问题。
Within each perimeter, you largely have control of your cables, devices, and computers because of physical controls, so you don’t need to worry as much about protection.

但要想做任何有用的事情，你必须在安全边界之间建立连接，这会将你暴露在各种你无法控制的电缆、设备和计算机之下。密码学是应对这种威胁最常见、最有效的控制手段。
But to do anything useful, you have to make connections between security perimeters, which exposes you to all sort of cables, devices, and computers you can’t control. Cryptography is the most common and useful control for addressing this threat.

Fire Wall

A device that filters all traffic between a protected or “inside” network and less trustworthy or “outside" network
用于过滤受保护的设备和不可信网络之间所有流量的设备

  • Most firewalls run as dedicated devices
  • Easier to design correctly and inspect for bugs
  • Easier to optimize for performance

大多数防火墙作为专用设备运行

  • 更易于正确设计和检查错误
  • 更易于性能优化

Firewalls implement security policies, or set of rules that determine what traffic can or cannot pass through A firewall is an example of a reference monitor, which means it should have three characteristics:

  • Always invoked (cannot be circumvented)
  • Tamperproof
  • Small and simple enough for rigorous analysis

防火墙会实施安全策略或规则集，以确定哪些流量可以通过或不能通过。防火墙是参考监视器的一个例子，这意味着它应该具备三个特点：

  • 始终被调用（无法规避）
  • 防篡改
  • 足够小巧简单，易于进行严格分析

What Firewalls Can and Cannot Do

  • Firewalls can protect an environment only if they control the entire perimeter

防火墙只有控制整个边界才能保护环境。

  • Firewalls do not protect data outside the perimeter

防火墙无法保护边界外的数据。

  • Firewalls are the most visible part of an installation to the outside, so they are an attractive target for attack

防火墙是系统对外最显眼的部分，因此很容易成为攻击目标。

  • Firewalls must be correctly configured, that configuration must be updated as the environment changes, and firewall activity reports must be reviewed periodically for evidence of attempted or successful intrusion

防火墙必须正确配置，并且必须随着环境变化而更新配置，并且必须定期审查防火墙活动报告，以查找入侵企图或成功入侵的证据。

  • Firewalls exercise only minor control over the content admitted to the inside, meaning that inaccurate or malicious code must be controlled by means inside the perimeter

防火墙对允许进入内部的内容的控制有限，这意味着必须通过边界内部的手段来控制不准确或恶意的代码。

IDS：Intrusion Detection Systems

入侵检测系统是预防性控制措施的下一道防线，通过监视活动以识别恶意可疑事件
IDSs complement preventative controls as a next line of defense. IDSs monitor activity to identify malicious or suspicious events.

IDSs may:

  • Monitor user and system activity

监控用户和系统活动

  • Audit system configurations for vulnerabilities and misconfigurations

审计系统配置中的漏洞和错误配置

  • Assess integrity of critical system and data files.

评估关键系统和数据文件的完整性

  • Recognize known attack patterns in system activity.

识别系统活动中已知的攻击模式

  • ldentify abnormal activity through statistical analysis.

管理审计线索并突出显示策略违规行为

  • Manage audit trails and highlight policy violations

通过统计分析识别异常活动

  • Install and operate traps to record information about intruders(or other attackers )

安装并运行陷阱以记录有关入侵者（或其他攻击者）的信息

总结

入侵检测系统的主要任务:操作系统或者网络行为的审计跟踪管理，并识别用户违反安全策略的行为总结
Main tasks of intrusion detection system: Audit tracking management of operating system or network behavior, and identification of user behavior that violates security policies

防火墙只是防御为主，通过防火墙的数据便不再进行任何操作，IDS则进行实时的检测，发现入侵行为即可做出反应，是对防火墙弱点的修补
Firewalls are mainly defense, and data passing through the firewall will no longer be operated. IDS performs real-time detection and can respond** when intrusion behavior is found, which is a patch for firewall weaknesses

防火墙可以允许内部的一些主机被外部访问， IDS则没有这些功能，只是监视和分析用户和系统活动。

Firewalls can allow **some internal hosts to be accessed from the outside, while IDS does not have these functions, and only monitors and analyzes user and system activities.

WEP Weakness

Weak encryption key
弱加密密钥

  • WEP allows to be either 64- or 128-bit, but 24 of those bits are reserved for initialization vectors (IV), thus reducing effective key size to 40 or 104 bits

  • WEP可以是64位或128位，但其中24位被保留用于初始化向量（IV），因此有效密钥大小减少到40位或104位

  • Keys were either alphanumeric or hex phrases that users typed in and were therefore vulnerable to dictionary attacks

  • 密钥是用户输入的字母数字或十六进制短语，因此容易受到字典攻击

Static key
静态密钥

  • Since the key was just a value the user typed in at the client and AP, and since users rarely changed those keys, one key would be used for many months of communications
  • 由于密钥只是用户在客户端和接入点（AP）输入的一个值，并且用户很少更改这些密钥，因此同一个密钥可能会用于许多个月的通信

Weak encryption process
弱加密过程

  • A 40-bit key can be brute forced easily. Flaws that were eventually discovered in the RC4 encryption algorithm WEP uses made the 104-bit keys easy to crack as well.
  • 40位密钥可以轻松进行暴力破解。WEP使用的RC4加密算法中的缺陷最终被发现，也使得104位密钥同样容易破解。

Weak encryption algorithm
弱加密算法

  • WEP used RC4 in a strange way (always a bad sign), which resulted in a flaw that allowed attackers to decrypt large portions of any WEP communication
    WEP以一种奇怪的方式使用了RC4（这通常是个坏兆头），导致了一个缺陷，使得攻击者能够解密任何WEP通信的大部分内容

IV collisions
IV碰撞

  • There were only 16 million possible values of IV, which, in practice, is not that many to cycle through for cracking. Also, they were not as randomly selected as they should have been, with some values being much more common than others
    IV只有1600万个可能的值，在实际应用中，循环破解的数量并不多。此外，IV的选择并不像应有的那样随机，其中一些值比其他值更常见

Faulty integrity check
错误的完整性检查

  • WEP messages included a checksum to identify transmission errors but did not use one that could address malicious modification
    WEP消息包括了一个校验和，用于识别传输错误，但没有使用能够处理恶意修改的校验和

No entity (user) authentication
没有实体（用户）认证

  • Only machine-to-machine authentication via shared key
    只有通过共享密钥的机器对机器认证

  • ny client that knows the AP’s SSID and MAC address is assumed to be legitimate
    任何知道接入点（AP）SSID和MAC地址的客户端都被假定为合法

WPA

Strong encryption
强加密

  • WPA adds support for AES, a much more reliably strong encryption algorithm
    WPA增加了对AES的支持，这是一种更可靠的强加密算法

Integrity protection
完整性保护

  • WPA includes a 64-bit cryptographic integrity check (MAC)
    WPA包括64位的密码学完整性检查（MAC）

Session initiation
会话初始化

  • Authenticated key exchange → session key
    认证密钥交换 → 会话密钥

  • WPA sessions begin with authentication and a four-way handshake that results in separate keys for encryption and integrity on both ends
    WPA会话以认证和四次握手开始，从而在两端生成用于加密和完整性的独立密钥

While there are some attacks against WPA, they are either of very limited effectiveness or require weak passwords
尽管WPA存在一些攻击，但它们要么效果非常有限，要么需要弱密码

DOS(Denial of Service)

拒绝式服务：一种使计算机系统和网络无法向用户提供正常服务的供给方式

  • Volumetric attacks容量耗尽攻击
  • Application-based attacks 应用攻击
  • Disabled communications（通信中断）：网络无法提供正常服务或资源访问，使目标系统服务器停止响应甚至崩溃
  • Hardware or software failure（硬件或软件故障）：

High-level Ideas of DoS Attacks

产生大量无用的网络协议数据流

  • 导致被攻击主机的网络拥塞，使被攻击主机无法正常与外界通信

Create a large flow of useless data of network protocols

  • causing network congestion leading to the attacked host, making it impossible for the attacked host to communicate with the outside world normally

利用传输或应用协议的漏洞

  • 反复发送畸形攻击数据，导致系统错误分配大量系统资源，造成主机挂起甚至崩溃
  • 伪造协议消息，使原有服务失效

Utilize the vulnerabilities of transmission or application protocol

  • repeated sending of malformed attack data causes the system to allocate a large amount of system resources incorrectly, causing the host to hang or even crash
  • forge protocol messages, invalidate the original services

PS:What are the other harms of DoS attacks from the perspectives of victims?
从受害者角度来看，DoS攻击还有哪些其他危害？

从受害者的角度来看，DoS攻击带来的主要危害包括经济损失和品牌声誉受损。攻击使服务中断，导致收入损失，特别是对于依赖线上交易的企业。此外，长期无法恢复服务还会导致客户流失，降低用户对品牌的信任，影响企业的市场地位。
此外，DoS攻击还可能暴露系统的安全漏洞，增加数据泄露和非法访问的风险。企业需投入大量资源进行修复和恢复，浪费了宝贵的计算资源，并可能面临合规性和法律问题，进一步增加了企业的负担。
From the perspective of the victims, the main harms caused by DoS attacks include economic losses and damage to brand reputation. The attack disrupted services and led to revenue losses, especially for enterprises that rely on online transactions. In addition, the long-term inability to restore services will also lead to customer loss, reduce users’ trust in the brand, and affect the market position of the enterprise.
In addition, DoS attacks may also expose security vulnerabilities in the system, increasing the risk of data leakage and illegal access. Enterprises need to invest a large amount of resources in repair and recovery, which wastes valuable computing resources and may face compliance and legal issues, further increasing the burden on enterprises.

DoS 攻击是一种网络攻击方式，攻击者通过向目标系统发送大量的无效请求或消耗资源，使其无法响应正常用户的请求，导致服务中断或变慢。

攻击者使用一台计算机，对目标服务器进行持续、密集的操作，例如：

  • 不断发送大量网络请求；
  • 占用服务器CPU、内存、带宽；
  • 请求某个功能却不完成，拖住资源（例如不完成握手）；

常见的 DoS 攻击类型：

  1. Ping Flood:利用ping命令不停发送ICMP请求，占用目标带宽和处理资源。
  2. SYN Flood:伪造大量TCP连接请求（SYN包），但不完成三次握手，占用服务器连接池。
  3. HTTP Flood:模拟用户浏览网页，不断访问网站页面，使Web服务器负载过高。
  4. DNS Spoofing：也称为 DNS 缓存污染，攻击者通过伪造 DNS 响应来将域名解析指向错误的 IP 地址，从而截获或篡改流量。

DDOS（分布式拒绝服务）

Distributed Denial of Service
是指攻击者通过多个分布在不同网络或设备上的攻击源（如被控制的机器、代理服务器等），同时向目标服务器、网络或服务发送大量请求或恶意报文，以致目标无法正常响应合法用户的请求，最终导致服务中断或严重延迟
It means that the attacker sends a large number of requests or malicious messages to the target server, network or service at the same time through multiple attack sources (such as controlled machines, proxy servers, etc.) distributed on different networks or devices, so that the target cannot normally respond to the requests of legitimate users, eventually causing service interruption or serious delay

常见类型：

  • 流量型攻击：如UDP洪水、ICMP洪水，通过发送海量数据占满带宽。
  • 连接耗尽型攻击：如SYN Flood，伪造TCP连接请求，占用服务器连接资源。
  • 应用层攻击：如HTTP Flood，看起来像正常访问，但数量巨大，消耗应用层资源。

模拟真实用户不停刷新页面（HTTP Flood）；不断请求建立连接却从不完成（三次握手不全，SYN Flood）；发大量无意义的数据，占满带宽（UDP Flood）；
Simulate real users constantly refreshing the page (HTTP Flood); constantly requesting to establish a connection but never completing it (incomplete three-way handshake, SYN Flood); sending a large amount of meaningless data and occupying the bandwidth (UDP Flood);

后果：

  • 网站瘫痪，无法访问
  • 业务中断，经济损失
  • 品牌声誉受损

应对方法：

  • 使用防火墙、WAF等安全设备
  • 启用DDoS防护服务（如Cloudflare、阿里云盾等）
  • 做好流量监控与应急预案
特征 DoS（Denial of Service） DDoS（Distributed Denial of Service）
攻击源数量 单一设备发起攻击 来自多个设备（成百上千，分布式）
攻击强度 相对较弱（容易被阻挡） 更强大，更难防御
可追踪性 较容易追踪攻击源 攻击源分布广，追踪困难
攻击方式 单个主机反复请求 僵尸网络统一控制，发起海量请求
典型攻击工具 ping、flood工具等简单脚本 使用botnet、控制多个被感染设备
影响范围 一般较小，持续时间短 影响大，持续时间长，可导致严重宕机

What Is Privacy?

什么是隐私？

Privacy is control over your own information.Freedom from intrusion into personal matters

隐私是指对自己信息的掌控，即个人事务不受侵犯的自由。

Privacy is a person’s right or expectation to control the show of his/her personal information,including activity metadata

隐私是指个人对其个人信息（包括活动元数据）披露的控制权或期望。

Types of data many people consider private:

  • Identity
  • Finances
  • Health
  • Biometrics
  • Privileged communications
  • Location data

隐私信息包括：身份信息、财务信息、健康信息、生物识别信息、特权通信信息、位置数据

Subject: person or entity being described by the data

主体：数据所描述的个人或实体

Owner: person or entity that holds the data

所有者：持有数据的个人或实体
